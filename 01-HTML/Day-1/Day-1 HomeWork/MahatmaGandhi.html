Mahatma Gandhi

Known for his philosophy of nonviolence (*ahimsa*), he inspired movements for civil rights and freedom across the globe. Below is comprehensive information about his life, achievements, and legacy:


Early Life

Birth: October 2, 1869, in Porbandar, Gujarat, India.

Parents:

Father: Karamchand Gandhi, a chief minister.

Mother: Putlibai, a deeply religious woman.

Education: Studied law in London and became a barrister in 1891.



Freedom Struggle and Contributions

Nonviolent Movements:

Championed movements like the Salt March, Quit India Movement, and Non-Cooperation Movement using nonviolence and civil disobedience.

Social Reforms:

Fought against untouchability, promoted rural development, and encouraged the use of khadi (hand-spun cloth).

Legacy:

Revered as the "Father of the Nation," Gandhi's principles of nonviolence inspire leaders worldwide.

Assassinated on January 30, 1948, leaving behind a legacy of peace and justice.
