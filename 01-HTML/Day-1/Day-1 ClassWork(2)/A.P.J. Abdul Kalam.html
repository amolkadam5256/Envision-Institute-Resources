Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam (1931–2015), widely known as the "Missile Man of India," was a renowned Indian aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India. Revered as an inspirational figure, he is celebrated for his contributions to science, education, and nation-building. Below is comprehensive information about his life, achievements, and legacy:


Early Life

Birth: October 15, 1931, at Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India.

Parents:

Father: Jainulabdeen, a boat owner and imam at a local mosque.

Mother: Ashiamma, a homemaker known for her kindness and simplicity.

Education: Kalam pursued his early education in Rameswaram and later graduated in physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli. He earned his degree in aerospace engineering from Madras Institute of Technology (MIT).





Scientific and Administrative Achievements

Contributions to India's Defense and Space Programs:

Played a key role in the development of ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology in India.

Worked with organizations like the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO).

Key Projects:

SLV-III (1980): Led the project that launched India's first satellite, Rohini, into orbit.

Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme (IGMDP): Developed missiles like Agni and Prithvi, earning him the title "Missile Man of India."

Pokhran-II Tests (1998): Played a significant role in India’s nuclear weapons tests, showcasing the country’s self-reliance in nuclear technology.

Vision for Development:

Advocated for India's transformation into a developed nation by 2020, outlined in his book *"India 2020: A Vision for the New Millennium."*

Championed the use of technology for societal development, focusing on healthcare, agriculture, and education.

Presidency:

Served as the 11th President of India (2002–2007), earning the nickname "People's President" for his accessibility and humility.

Promoted youth empowerment, innovation, and national integration.

Legacy:

Authored several inspirational books, including *"Wings of Fire"* and *"Ignited Minds."*

Passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management (IIM) Shillong, continuing to inspire until his last moment.
