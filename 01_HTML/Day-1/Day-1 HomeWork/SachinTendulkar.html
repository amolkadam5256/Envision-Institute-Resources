Sachin Tendulkar

Sachin Ramesh Tendulkar, born in 1973, is one of the greatest cricketers in history. Known as the "God of Cricket," he holds numerous records and is an inspiration to millions. Below is comprehensive information about his life, achievements, and legacy:


Early Life

Birth: April 24, 1973, in Mumbai, Maharashtra, India.

Parents:

Father: Ramesh Tendulkar, a Marathi novelist.

Mother: Rajni Tendulkar, worked in the insurance sector.

Education: Attended Sharadashram Vidyamandir, where he began training under coach Ramakant Achrekar.



Cricketing Achievements

Key Records:

The first cricketer to score 100 international centuries.

Holds the record for the highest runs in Test and ODI cricket.

World Cup:

Played six World Cups and was part of the winning team in 2011.

Legacy:

Awarded the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honor, in 2014.

Continues to inspire budding cricketers worldwide.
