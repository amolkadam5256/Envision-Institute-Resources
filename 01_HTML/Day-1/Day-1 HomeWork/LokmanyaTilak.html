Revered as "Lokmanya," meaning "accepted by the people," he was a pioneer in nationalist awakening. Below is comprehensive information about his life, achievements, and legacy:


Early Life

Birth: July 23, 1856, in Ratnagiri, Maharashtra, India.

Parents:

Father: Gangadhar Tilak, a Sanskrit scholar and teacher.

Mother: Parvatibai Tilak, a devoted homemaker.

Education: Graduated from Deccan College, Pune, with a degree in mathematics and law.



Contributions to Indian Independence

Advocacy for Swaraj:

Popularized the slogan *"Swaraj is my birthright and I shall have it,"* igniting the freedom movement.

Role in Publications:

Founded and edited two influential newspapers: *Kesari* (Marathi) and *The Mahratta* (English), promoting nationalist ideas.

Social Reforms:

Revived traditional festivals like Ganesh Chaturthi and Shivaji Jayanti to unite Indians and foster a sense of cultural identity.

Legacy:

Known as the "Father of Indian Unrest" by the British, Tilak inspired many future leaders like Mahatma Gandhi.

Passed away on August 1, 1920, but remains a symbol of courage and patriotism.
